\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Probe: the anticipation-value chain}
\label{sec:probe-results}

Table~\ref{tab:probe} reports the controlled probe (\S\ref{sec:experiments}) on
Greek$\rightarrow$English TEDx segments with English slides (M3 regime, human
references, $n{=}40$; Qwen3-32B translator, Local Agreement policy). The
\emph{hard stratum} contains segments where the audio-only baseline misses most
reference terminology (term recall $\leq 0.4$; $n{=}22$).

\toprule

\midrule
A\; audio-only                & 38.8 & ---    & ---   & ---     \\
B\; slide terms (OCR)         & 35.2 & $-2.9$ & n.s.  & $+2.3$  \\

B\; VLM + LLM-gate            & 38.0 & $-2.0$ & n.s.  & $+10.1$ \\

\paragraph{The premise holds, and gains sit exactly where predicted.}
Injecting the reference's own rare terms (C) --- a perfect-anticipation oracle
--- yields $+7.0$ chrF pooled and $+18.8$ on the hard stratum
($p{<}10^{-4}$), with hard-stratum term recall rising
$0.12\!\rightarrow\!0.41$ ($3.4\times$). Gains concentrate on segments where
the baseline fails on terminology and are neutral elsewhere --- the signature
of anticipation value rather than generic prompt enrichment. The effect
replicates across model scales (7B pilot) and both language regimes (zh
secondary set: $+6.0$ pooled, term recall $0.52\!\rightarrow\!0.68$).

\paragraph{Extraction quality is load-bearing.}
OCR slide terms are neutral-to-harmful (fragments such as
``\textit{independen}'' from 480p projections), while VLM-read terms recover
$+14.1$ chrF on hard segments --- 75\% of the oracle's hard-stratum gain,
delivered by the \emph{real} visual channel.

\paragraph{Injection timing closes the rest.}
Ungated injection is flat pooled because easy segments are disturbed; gating
injection on segments that need it recovers $+7.3$ pooled ($p{<}0.001$),
statistically indistinguishable from --- and cleaner than --- the oracle-terms
condition. Runtime gates tested so far lack selectivity (an LLM relevance
gate fires on 88\% of segments; rule-based uncertainty signals either
under-fire retrospectively or over-fire, \S\ref{sec:gating}), identifying
learned failure prediction as the concrete open problem.

\paragraph{Bad evidence is benign at prompt level; easy-segment interference
is a protocol effect.}
Wrong-slide terms (D) are within noise of the baseline. Per-stratum analysis
shows the easy-stratum dips under \emph{all} hint conditions (including D) with
truncation signatures, tracing to Local Agreement's sensitivity to prompt
perturbations rather than term over-adoption; logits-level hint integration is
the indicated remedy for the full system.
